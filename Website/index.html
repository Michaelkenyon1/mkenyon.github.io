My Path

My path to where I am today hasn't been a straight line, and I wouldn’t have it any other way. I started out in the high-pressure world of professional kitchens in New York City, including my time training at the Culinary Institute of America. While the intensity was addictive, I realized that what kept me going wasn't just the food, it was the team dynamics and the challenge of creating a great experience for people.

That shift in perspective led me to the University of Dayton to pursue my B.A. in Communication Management. I’ve leaned into the PR and marketing side of the degree, focusing on how strategic messaging can move the needle for a brand. I’ve balanced a heavy course load with work to make sure I’m hitting my goals and staying on track to graduate.

Currently, I’m working as an HR Student Assistant at the university for the summer, where I manage database compliance and internal communication workflows. This fall, I’m moving into an Employer Relations internship, also here at UD, where I’ll be managing partnerships and bridging the gap between our academic programs and the outside industry.

When I graduate, I’m looking to bring that operational background into a corporate setting. I’m interested in the "behind-the-scenes" of a business, the communication and project work that keeps a team aligned and a brand’s story clear. I want to work with a team where I can use my experience to help build systems and tell stories that actually land with the people they’re meant to reach.
